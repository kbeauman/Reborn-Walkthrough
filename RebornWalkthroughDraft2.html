=====================

Before you begin the game, I highly advise reading the Hints section of the README_REBORN_253_ENG.txt file in the Reborn Team folder. My Windows 7 installation put the folder on my desktop. Here are a few hints of my own:

  • This game crashes a lot! Save often, especially after you score an important find or complete a difficult task. Also, Saves, especially Quick Saves, seem to get corrupted after you load them several times. If you're having a hard time with something and find yourself reloading several times, I suggest making a backup Save, i.e., load the Save, then Save again under another name.
  • I play this at either Stalker or Veteran level. If you play at Novice, you may find it easier to kill some mutants, encounter fewer Undefeatable Zombies, and fewer enemies in general. However, you may also find some of the items mentioned in this walkthrough, such as PDAs, are missing. Example: When you first arrive at the Garbage and free the hostages from the Vehicle graveyard, you may not get a reward.
  • The dialogs are, at times, mind-numbingly long in comparison to the original game. To make matters worse, the writing style is either an abysmal attempt at vernacular or we’re suppose to think all these characters flunked the second grade. Many of these conversations seem to have no bearing on the game, serve to be misleading information, or distractions from the main objectives.
  • Most Zombies can be taken out with a single shot gun to the head (pump, not that semi-auto piece of junk). Just don’t let them get too close. When there is a group of them, an RPG works in most cases. The flamethrower works, too. But I like to save that for the spiders.
  • The best weapon I’ve found for taking out Controllers, Burers (a.k.a. lawn gnomes), and Undefeatable Zombies (usually wearing an Exoskeleton suit) is the Barrett M82A1 (.50 cal). Again, head shot = one shot. You can also do it with about five or six rounds from the Dragunov, or several rounds from a Vintar.
  • Mutant spiders can’t climb very high. My favorite tactic is to get up on a vehicle or boulder and give them each about five shot gun blasts, a couple of VOG-25 grenades, or a blast from the flamethrower.
  • Bloodsuckers cannot be killed while invisible with most weapons. However, they show themselves just before they strike. So, if you’re up for a game of chicken, load up the shot gun and stand your ground. You may also want to save before and after the encounter.
  • I've added links to various pages of the S.T.A.L.K.E.R. Wiki: Clear Sky to provide more information. I've written quite a bit on the stashes, locations, and helped on some other pages.
  • Check every corpse, you never know what you might find.
  • Possible bug with traders: If you attempt a big trade with one of the traders and get a message that they don't have enough money, try one or two items at a time.

The Walkthrough

==============

As you can tell by the empty entries in the preceding lists, this is a work in progress. If you have any suggestions, things that I've missed, or questions, please send me a note: kwag_myers@att.net.

Zarton

The game starts with you spawning at sunset in the northwest corner of the Zarton map from Call of Pripyat (COP) and you instantly get a message to run. If you don’t, a mutant thorn bush makes you start the game all over. Sprint southeast toward the bridge, which is west the Burnt Farmstead, and then check the map when you get there. This is a good place to save, because if you get caught by a Psuedogiant you have to start all over again with a new game. Head southeast to the Skadovsk, which is marked on your map, and stay away from the Psuedogiants. They don't come after you unless you get too close.

Once at the Skadovsk you will notice that you can’t stand on the mess decking. This is important to know because it can work for you or against you, depending on where you are. This also is true for wire fences. Go inside, up the stairs and turn left to get the “swag”. You might as well put your weapons away because they are useless on this map.

If you want to explore the map, you may find a few corpses (with nothing on them) and the south end of the Preobrazhensky Bridge is surrounded by a teleport that takes you back to the complex on the north end of the bridge where there's spiders and Zombies. One video I watched shows an artifact at the south end of the bridge, but I haven't figured out how to get there without teleporting back to the north end. The player who made the video had an impressive inventory, so maybe we'll come back to this later?

The PDA shows only one transition point in the Testing Workshop of the Iron Forest (a.k.a. the Stingray 2 crash site), which is in the southwest corner of the map. The problem is, the place is infested with mutants. You don’t have enough ammo to kill them all, so a little game of Catch Me If You Can is your best option:

  • One tactic, which seems easy if you do it right, is to enter the area from the east. Walk toward the mutants until you hear them react, then run back to an opening in the wall as far south (right) as you can. Then head south around the shipping crates and to the far southeast corner of where the buildings along the south side of the complex extend out to the east. Wait for the mutants to be sure they don't lose your trail (otherwise you have to start all over again). When the first mutant gets close, walk through the fence and wait for the rest of them to join up. Again, the Psuedogiants are the slowest and once the first one gets there, sprint east before it stomps. Go west until you can get between the buildings and then head north to the end of the wall that runs along the west side of the Iron Forest. Carefully maneuver around the wall and sprint to the Testing Workshop.
  • Another tactic is to show yourself to the mutants at the north end of the complex, then run northeast to the Oakpine anomaly. This tactic is much riskier because if you stop while the mutants are chasing you, you're dead. Climb up and wait for all the mutants to get there. You don't need to go to the top, but just so you're over the gully to cause the mutants to go below you. I like to use the southwest leg of the anomaly as it's the shortest distance and easy to walk up. Once the first Psuedogiant is almost below you, sprint back to the Testing Workshop. Don't wait too long because the Psuedogiants will stomp and you won't be able to sprint for a few seconds, which is all the spiders need to catch you.
Note: This next part is complicated and you may be able to pause the game when you reach the bottom of the second set of stairs if you need to reference this section again.

As soon as you enter the door, turn left and go down the steps one level, then left through the doorway and follow the hall to the right. If you encounter a blind dog along the way, just keep running. If you're playing at the Master level, you may also have a black spider in your way. They usually take four to five shot gun blasts and you only have two in the chamber. You'll be dead before you can reload, so try to jump over it.

At the end of the hall, there's a quick right than left down two more sets of stairs at a right angle and continue down a longer hall (don't go all the way down the stairs, but just to the first doorway). At the end and to the left are four more sets of stairs. At the bottom of the stairs, go through the door on the right, and then left into the large room where the Psuedogiant was in Call Of Pripyat. Mounted to the wall on the left is a ladder you can climb to the top. Now you’re safe from the mutants and may want to quick save in case you fall transitioning to the cat walk. If you're bleeding and loosing health, take a vodka or two.

Condensed version: Left stairs, left door, right hall, right/left stairs, left stairs, right, left, ladder.

In Call Of Pripyat, there was an opening in the duct work in the southwest corner of the room opposite where you entered that led to the area where you found the papers for the Gauss rifle. This is where the transition point is located. Make your way over there using the overhead crane. In fact, you can stay on the cat walk of the crane to transition point without actually jumping down into the duct.

Yantar

At this point in the game, the map on your PDA has some added reference points. Some differ from the original Clear Sky and I will try to use this game's version to (hopefully) avoid confusion.

You transport to the Mobile Lab compound in Yantar and the screen displays a thought. This is your cue to make your way to the Clear Sky camp in the Swamp to get answers from Dr. Bashnya. There are two transition points to Agroprom, one to the south and another to the east (which is guarded by a group of Zombies). It doesn’t matter which one you take, but I recommend taking on the Zombies so you can start collecting weapons and ammo. Be aware that these guys are packin’ some heavy weaponry. But they're not too quick and you can run past them before they react. Then you'll have more cover, and if you're lucky, the zombie with the RPG will do your work for you. If not, you should have just enough shot gun ammo to take them out with head shots. Collect as much gear as you can, and notice that you can carry a whole lot more that your limit. At this point (without artifacts) you can carry 200 kilograms. Once you’re done with the Zombies, you may be tempted to go hunting Zombies for more loot. But there's plenty along the way, so I recommend you head for the transition point.

If you happen to score an RPG, be sure to save at least four rounds for later. There's a couple of helicopters coming up that you'll want to get rid of if you have the ammo.

Agroprom

If you used the southeast transition point, there are two corpses on top of the hill in front of you (the Stone Ring) with more loot. Walk between or around the two trees, up the slope and climb on top of the rocks to avoid the mutants. Also, avoid contact with Duty even though they show as neutral on your PDA, as they turn on you without warning. There are some Zombies in the area if you want more swag (remember: head shots save ammo). Also, if you happen to gain stash coordinates, you can leave them for now and collecting them later in the game. Especially if the stash is near a Duty post. By the time you get to a trader, you'll have more than you can carry anyway.

From the Stone Ring, head southeast to the Barracades by a train car, which may also have a few armed Zombies around. In the tower is a dead Bandit named Sheluden with the first PDA. Sheluden's PDA has some valuable information on flash drives and another PDA in the Underground, which we will get to later. Many of these flash drives contain information about hidden transition points. Getting off the top of the ladder can be tricky. Look up and to the left usually works for some reason. Turn around and walk to the outside of the platform, then along the south to the corpse and check your health (you probably need a vodka soon). Loot the corpse, get down as quick as you can and take another vodka if you need to. Then head for the northeast transition point to teleport to the Garbage. If you encounter Zombies with no weapons, just sprint past them.

Garbage

If you free the hostages at the Vehicle graveyard, you get a stash as a reward, which contains a very handy Vepr shotgun.

Head for the transition point near the Receiving Outpost at the south end of the road, again avoiding any contact. As you approach you'll hear a fire fight, which usually ends with an RPG blast. A squad of Russian Special Forces (RSF) is attacking some Bandits. You can either wait for the attackers to finish up and head toward the Gravel Pit, or try to take them out with an RPG as they leave. Either way, there's probably more loot than you can carry at this point.

Be sure to save before you move on and check your PDA, because you never know who or what is going to be waiting for you when you spawn at the Far Outpost. I've been greeted by mutants and a squad of RSF. I was able to fight my way past the RSF, but the mutants forced me to go back through the Garbage, Dark Valley, and then to the Cordon.

Cordon North

From the Far Outpost, you may encounter a Controller having lunch on a corpse near the Camp Under Rocks. If you are lucky enough to score a .50 cal, take the head shot. Or, a Dragunov does the job with five head shots. Otherwise, go east through the fence and keep your distance. As you head south toward the Northern Farmstead in the Cordon there are mutants, another Controller, and two Burers on the west side of the road near the Bivouac in the Meadow. This is a fight you don't need to start, so it's best to avoid them. You may notice two transition points to the west of Hideout Under Rocks. These are described here.

More corpses near Camp in Spruce Forest guarded by unarmed Zombies. You should be able out maneuver them and save some ammo. Sheluden's PDA mentions a flash drive at the Quiet Hideout, which is east of the Old Farmstead. The corpse is guarded by some Psuedodogs and at least one of them is a cloner. It's best to take them out from a distance before they start cloning to save ammo. I should mention that clones can go through fences, too. The body of the RSF has a flamethrower and the Flash drive with data of hidden transition point on the Swamps. Get to the Northern Farmstead so you can trade in all your goodies and get your gear repaired. You can take on optional missions, but there's no flash drives or PDAs in it for you. And unless you are really hard up for cash, these are a waste of time.

From the Quiet Hideout, go west to the Bonfire in Forest, but not to the transition point. There's a helicopter that likes to shoot rockets at you when you're in the Village Center. From the Bonfire in Forest, go up the hill to the railroad tracks and follow them west until you can get a good shot with an RPG. If you're luck enough to hit it in the tail section, it will go down with one shot. Otherwise, you're going to need a second hit. Once that's out of the way, follow the western edge of the map heading south towards the transition point in the southwest part of the map. As you pass the Bonfire Under Tree, be sure to stay on the east side of the fence. There's a hidden teleport up on the hill that we don't want to use just yet.

Go straight to the transition point and do NOT stop at Sidorovich's. You may only get one chance to visit Sidorovich, and if you go in now, you will probably wish you hadn't later. Go outside the fence when you get close to the tunnel so the military doesn't start shooting. Go into the tunnel and teleport to the Swamp.

Swamp

You spawn on the east side of the Southern Farmstead, which has a few Lone Wolfs that you can either avoid or take out. Then head for the Clear Sky camp by following the fence that runs south of the Southern Farmstead. As you approach the gate, you’ll be stopped by a rather length dialog, at which time you can choose how you want to play the rest of the game. My choice for this walkthrough is to join Clear Sky. Here are the objectives:

  • Find the Art-hunter and buy artifacts.
  • Find the way to Limansk.
  • Find all three Artifacts Of Death.
  • Collect 12 PDAs.

I should also mention that I found playing the game with emissions on only slows the game down and offers no additional change your playing abilities, only to your patience. Also, Lebedev tells you about using Esc+F2, which brings up the Faction Commander control panel. This may be helpful when you're ordered to capture and hold a point on the map, but don't have any support from Clear Sky. You can use the Faction Commander to hire some help, or capture it on your own and hire a squad to hold (defend) the position.

Notes: There are two teleports in Clear Sky's camp. One is behind the trader's counter and takes you near the Camp Amidst Some Rocks in the Dark Valley. You have to jump up on the counter, then crouch-walk to get to it. The other teleport is in the tree next to the technician's hut, and usually takes you to the top of the Flea Market. However, I have ended up southwest of the Pump Station once (I think it depends on where you are in the game as to the destination). To use it, go up the stairs of the hut, turn right and go up the ramp to the roof of the building east of the hut. Then, jump to the front part of the hut's roof and sprint-jump at the tree.
I wasn't able to find a transition point mentioned in the flash drive. I checked the graves next to the old church and the graves just north of there. I looked at several locations for a small grave yard, but the only one I found is east of the Clear Sky base. However, the grave yard next to the church is the landing spot for the secret transition point behind the Duty base in the Agroprom (maybe that's it?). More information here.

Don’t forget to cash in any weapons you collected from the Lone Wolfs and get your reward. Also, make sure your gear is in good shape. Novicov is the only technician that can install night vision on Clear Sky suits (if you happen to have one), so get it fitted before you leave the Swamp. Be sure to talk to Lotsman, the guide at the south end of the camp. He gives you some information on transition points (which I haven’t quite figured out how to use yet). He also agrees to meet you in Limansk, which is an important part of the game.

Once you’re ready to leave the camp, check your PDA for a red target west by northwest of the camp. This is Meatgrinder, and that’s your next objective. After talking with him, head for the watchtower on the west edge of the map, where in the original Clear Sky, you were sent on your first mission. The guy in the tower tells you to run for the tower and when you do, he climbs down and gets killed. Once you clear out the mutants, be sure to get his Monstrpress shot gun, which can be used as a secondary weapon. There's a Pseudogiant in the area which desided to wait for me at the base of the tower once. I was able to kill it with a single RPG round to the head.

There's another helicopter that flies along the northern and northwestern edge of the Swamp, and attacts places along the north, like the Village Ruins and Northern Farmstead. On a clear day, you can hit the helicopter with an RPG from the watchtower. If you're low on RPG rounds and happen to have a minigun, this helicopter stops and turns around northwest of Boulder Island where you can take it down with about 200-300 rounds.

You now have an Echo detector and can start collecting artifacts. From the tower, there's an artifact to the south, then head north to the area marked Detector "Veles" on your PDA map. There's two more artifacts along the way. See Clear Sky Artifacts for a map. Be sure to collect as many artifacts as you can and read the description of each one. Some artifacts can be used to create a Gravi anomaly (a.k.a. Springboard) and you should save them. To use an artifact, put it in the secondary weapon spot and hold the shoot key/button for a few seconds to deploy. You may want to practice a few times to get the timing down. The downside to using artifacts is when one of your allies gets killed by the anomaly, his friends turn on you. Usually, just those in his squad and the rest of the faction stays friendly.

Remember that Veles behind the mattress in the original Clear Sky? If not, there’s a cave between the box cars and bridge, and behind the mattress in the corner is a Veles detector. See Hidden Veles Detector if you need a walkthrough. And be sure to check the cave for an artifact before you leave. You may pick up a Gravi that's not on the aforementioned Clear Sky Artifacts map.

At this point, you can hang around the Swamp as long as you like, maybe take some jobs and build up your inventory of ammo and cash. You can buy stashes from the trader and barkeep, but none of them have flash drives or PDAs. I like to capture all the points on the map, then spawn a squad using the Faction Commander set at Expansion to hold the point. This reduces the number of messages you get when you leave the Swamp about Clear Sky objectives that you really don't need to be concerned with. Otherwise, the Lone Wolfs move right back in and you're constantly getting mission objectives for the Swamp throughout the rest of the game. Or, you can just head for the Northern Farmstead and ignore the messages.

Up in the northeast corner of the map, east of the Northern Farmstead, is a Person of Interest. This is Zarka, and she will ask you to escort her to her friends, which are northwest of the Encampment Under Embankment. Before you contact Zarka, you have to clear out the Northern Farmstead. When you do, be sure to watch your PDA for reinforcements coming in from the southwest. If you look out the western facing window of the house you can see the bridge they use to get to the farm.

Take Zarka to her friends (who may not actually be there, but it doesn't matter). Be sure to keep her on the minimap or she’ll go back to her starting point. She stops by the pond just west of the Northern Farmstead to check for anomalies, then goes west until the fence turns north toward the train wreck. She passes the train wreck on the north side and meets up with her buddies just a little further west. After the dialog, they head to Agroprom, but don’t bother trying to follow them.

When you're ready to move on, ask a guide to take you to the Village Center, or head for the transition point in the northeast corner of the map (where you met Zarka).

Cordon South

If you used the transition point, you spawn in the Cordon just south of the Military Outpost. Walk on the east side of the fence until you get past the mine field. Go see Wolf in the Village Center and talk to him about helping a stalker (named Anelia) from the tunnel to the Dark Valley, which is marked Bonfire by Tunnel on your PDA map. Anelia is trapped by three or four Bloodsuckers running around the camp. This is not actually a job with the objective listed in your PDA or marked on the map. But it is necessary to gain one of the Artifacts of Death. But first you have to clear the way, or you'll have a tough time completing the mission. There's a Burer and some mutants at, or north of, the Camp Near Van just north of the Village Center. Then head for the Vehicle Station, where you have to take out the RSF there in order to clear the way for Anelia.

The easiest way I've found to clear out the Vehicle Station is to use the bus stop as cover and snipe as many as you can. If you're luck, a bunch of them will come out the front gate where you can take them out with an RPG. Sometimes one or two of them stay inside the buildings and you have to go in to finish clearing them out. Then head east to the Bonfire by Tunnel.

My tactic for dealing with these Bloodsuckers is to stand in the road just west of the rail fence. This seems to be their boundary for some reason and you can step forward a few steps to bait them to come at you. Remember to wait until they show themselves just before striking and take your shot. Oh, and you have to give Anelia a gun before she will leave. In one game, Anelia was actually west of the camp and safe from the Bloodsuckers. Still, I had to clear them out and wait for her to go sit in the shelter before getting her to leave. Like Zarka, you must keep Anelia on your minimap as you escort her to the Village Center, or you'll have to start the mission all over again.

Note: I noticed that when I tried to quick save and go back to that point, Anelia might return to the starting point. Regular saves seem to be safe.

When you get back to the camp, Anelia tells you all about looking for her father and how her boyfriend came to the Zone to make some money for their wedding, but became a zombie instead. Let her tell her story because you get coordinates for a stash when she's done. Then go get the stash and the nearby artifact. See the walkthrough for Reward for a Hero if you need help getting the artifact. In the stash is the Joker of Horror flash drive that tells you to look up Korsakov, who is in the Agroprom.

Go back to the Village Center and find Anelia, who is hold up in the underground storm cellar. Offer to take her to her father and she leads the way, leaving the camp on the west end, over the hill to the Bonfire Under Tree, through the fence (I guess she can do the fence thing too) and up the hill toward the hidden transition point. Then she turns around and goes back past the east entrance to the Village Center, through the tunnel under the railroad tracks, past the Encampment in Trough and stops just before the two transition points. If you haven't taken care of the two mutants here, you may want to sprint ahead and do that before she gets there (just don't run into the transition point). When Anelia stops, she tells you to meet her in the Agroprom, then turns and disappears.

Now head for the Dark Valley by way of the tunnel where you rescued Anelia from the Bloodsuckers.

Dark Valley

There may be a squad of Mercenaries at the Encampment on Farm in the southwestern corner of the map. If so, they may fire on you and they may not, so save your game before you approach them. Collect Commandant Glykin's PDA, which was Lingov's corpse in the original Clear Sky. This PDA tells you of the 12 PDAs you must collect. The three in the Dark Valley are in the same places as the original Clear Sky. You have one, another at the Encampment at Foothill, and the last one on top of the large storage tank at the Gas Station.

There is a Controller in the Camp amidst some rocks, as well as a Burer and one or two Psuedogiants on the south side of the Freedom base. You can probably avoid the Controller, but I like to take out the Burer and Psuedogiant when I'm done at the Freedom base to make life easier later on. Some Psuedogiants go down with a single RPG while others take four or five. I haven't figured out how to tell the difference yet. You can also use an artifact. Ideally, you should drop the artifact a few yards from the southern wall of the Freedom base, about half way between the overpass and the road where there's a short wall running north and south with an opening in the middle (that's the spot). The Freedom guys don't usually go in that area.

From the Encampment on Farm, you can collect the PDAs and artifacts any way you want. I prefer to go east to the Fault anomaly, then to the Springboard anomaly at the southeast corner of the Factory Lobby. This anomaly seems to be buggy, even in the original Clear Sky. I find that sometimes I can get the artifact with no issues by just walking along the southern wall of the factory. Other times, the artifact shows on my detector, but I'm unable to grab it. I've found that if I close the game and reboot, I can then get the artifact. I've even had instances where I've quit for the day and shut down my machine, I can easily grab the artifact the next time I boot the game up. Or, just wait until later in the game, because we'll be back to the Dark Valley a few more times. If you need any help with the stashes, see Clear Sky Stashes by Location - Dark Valley for walkthroughs.

From the factory, go along the east side of the wall to the Encampment at Foothill to pick up the PDA los(sic) in Valley laying on a boulder with a mattress against it. Again, there may be a Mercenary or two guarding the camp that you'll have to take out first.

Next, make your way to the gas station and get the PDA from the corpse on top of the tank, which is (Uncle Artikular friend's PDA). This is where you went to get the flash drive off of Yar’s friend in the original Clear Sky. If you need help getting the artifact from the top of the north tank, see the Note section for Gas Station. This PDA mentions a teleport in a tube at the Mercenary's base, which is marked on your map as the Factory Lobby. But if you try it too soon, the teleport is disabled.

You can go inside the Freedom base now, or wait until you collect the next artifact. The base is under attact by Zombies and you have to clear them out before Ashot the trader appears. Then go to the northwest corner of the camp wall and go west to the fence. Follow the fence north past a bush until, using your minimap, you are directly in line with the two corpses. Take out your detector and move in to grab the artifact, and loot the bodies if you want. Then, turn and go out the same way you came in.

Hidden transition point: North of the Stalker's grave stash that randomly goes to the Bonfire under tree in the Cordon, the factory in Yantar, northeast corner of Agroprom, and the Hide Near Path in the Garbage (which is usually occupied by Bandits, and they are not happy to see you).
