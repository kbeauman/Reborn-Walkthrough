=====================

Before you begin the game, I highly advise reading Hints section of the README_REBORN_253_ENG.txt file in the Reborn Team folder. My Windows 7 installation put the folder on my desktop. Here are a few hints of my own:

  • This game crashes a lot! Save often, especially after you score an important find or complete a difficult task. For me, a crash usually happens with the Inventory open for more than a few seconds or when transition pointing.
  • I play this at either Stalker or Veteran level. If you play at Novice, you may find it easier to kill some mutants, encounter fewer Undefeatable Zombies, and fewer enemies in general.
  • The dialogs are, at times, mind-numbingly long in comparison to the original game. To make matters worse, the writing style is either an abysmal attempt at vernacular or we're suppose to think all these characters flunked the second grade. Many of these conversations seem to have no bearing on the game. At this point in the game, they seem to be distractions from the main objectives.
  • Most zombies can be taken out with a single shot gun to the head (pump, not that semi-auto piece of junk). Just don't let them get too close. When there is a group of them, an RPG works in most cases. The flamethrower works, too. But I like to save that for the spiders.
  • The best weapon I've found for taking out Controllers, Burers (a.k.a. lawn gnomes), and Undefeatable Zombies (usually wearing Exoskeleton suit) is the Barrett M82A1 (.50 cal). Again, head shot = one shot. You can also do it with about five or six rounds from the Dragunov, or several rounds from a Vintar.
  • Mutant spiders can't climb very high. My favorite tactic is to get up on a vehicle or boulder and give them each about five shot gun blasts, a couple of VOG-25 grenades, or a blast from the flamethrower.
  • Bloodsuckers cannot be killed while invisible. At least, I haven't figured out a way to do it yet. However, they show themselves just before they strike. So, if you're up for a game of chicken, load up the shot gun and stand your ground. You may also want to save before and after the encounter.
  • I've added links to various pages of the S.T.A.L.K.E.R. Wiki: Clear Sky to provide more information. I've written quite a bit on the stashes, locations, and helped on some other pages.
  • Check every corpse, you never know what you might find.

The Walkthrough

==============

As you can tell by the empty entries in the preceding lists, this is a work in progress. There is still a lot of back and forth between maps (sorry) which I hope to reduce once I complete the game. If you have any suggestions, things that I've missed, or questions, please send me a note: kwag_myers@att.net.

Zarton

The game starts with you spawning at sunset in the northwest corner of the Zarton map from Call of Pripyat (COP) and you instantly get a message to run. If you don't, a mutant thorn bush makes you start the game all over, so best to run. Sprint southeast until you get to the Burnt Farmstead, and then check the map. This is also a good place to save, because if you get caught by the Psuedogiant you have to start all over again with a new game. You want to head straight for the Skadovsk, which is marked on your map.

Once at the Skadovsk you will notice that you can't stand on the mess decking. This is important to know because it can work for you or against you, depending on where you are. This also is true for wire fences. Go inside, up the stairs and turn left to get the “swag”. You might as well put your weapons away because they are useless on this map.

If you want to explore the map, you may find a few corpses (with nothing on them) and the south end of the Preobrazhensky Bridge is surrounded by a teleport that takes you back to the complex on the north end of the bridge where there's spiders and zombies. One video I watched shows an artifact at the south end of the bridge, but I haven't figured out how to get there without teleporting back to the north end. The player who made the video had 21 PDAs in their inventory, so maybe we'll come back to this later?

The PDA shows only one transition point in the Testing Workshop of the Iron Forest (a.k.a. the Stingray 2 crash site) in the southwest corner of the map. The problem is, the place is infested with mutants. You don't have enough ammo to kill them all, so a little game of Catch Me If You Can is your best option:

  • One tactic I found online is to show yourself to the mutants, then run to the Oakpine anomaly to the northeast. Climb up and wait for all the mutants to get there. You don't need to go to the top, but just so you're over the gully to cause the mutants to go below you. I like to use the southwest leg of the anomaly as it's the shortest distance and easy to walk up. Once the first Psuedogiant is almost below you, sprint back to the Testing Workshop. Don't wait too long because the Psuedogiants will stomp and you won't be able to sprint for a few seconds, which is all the spiders need to catch you.
  • Another tactic, which seems easier if you do it right, is to enter the area from the east. Walk toward the mutants until you hear them react, then run back to an opening in the wall as far south (right) as you can. Then head south around the shipping crates and to the southeast corner of where the building extends. Wait for the mutants to be sure they don't lose your trail (otherwise you have to start all over again). When the first mutant gets close, walk through the fence and wait for the rest of them to join up. Again, the Psuedogiants are the slowest and once the first one gets there, sprint east before it stomps. Go west until you can get between the buildings and then head north to the end of the wall that runs along the west side of the Iron Forest. When you get to the end of the wall, carefully maneuver around the wall and sprint to the Testing Workshop.

    • Note: This next part is complicated and you may be able to pause the game when you reach the bottom of the second set of stairs if you need to reference this section again.

    As soon as you enter the door, turn left and go down the steps one level, then left through the doorway and follow the hall to the right. If you encounter a blind dog along the way, just keep running. If you're playing at the Master level, you may also have a black spider in your way. They usually take four to five shot gun blasts and you only have two in the chamber. You'll be dead before you can reload, so try to jump over it.

    At the end of the hall, there's a quick right than left down two more sets of stairs at a right angle and continue down a longer hall (don't go all the way down the stairs, but just to the first doorway). At the end and to the left are four more sets of stairs. At the bottom of the stairs, go through the door on the right, and then left into the large room where the pseudogiant was in Call Of Pripyat. Mounted to the wall on the left is a ladder you can climb to the top. Now you're safe from the mutants and may want to quick save in case you fall transitioning to the cat walk. If you're bleeding and loosing health, take a vodka or two.

    Condensed version: Left stairs, left door, right hall, right/left stairs, left stairs, right, left, ladder.

    In Call Of Pripyat, there was an opening in the duct work in the southwest corner of the room opposite where you entered that led to the area where you found the papers for the Gauss rifle. This is where the transition point is located. Make your way over there using the overhead crane. In fact, you can stay on the cat walk of the crane to transition point without actually jumping down into the duct.

    Yantar

    At this point in the game, the map on your PDA has some added reference points. Some differ from the original Clear Sky and I will try to use this game's version to (hopefully) avoid confusion.

    You transport to the Mobile Lab compound in Yantar and the screen displays a thought. This is your cue to make your way to the Clear Sky camp in the Swamp to get answers from Dr. Bashnya. There are two transition points to Agroprom, one to the south and another to the east (which is guarded by a group of zombies). It doesn't matter which one you take, but I recommend taking on the zombies so you can start collecting weapons. Be aware that these guys are packin'. But they're not too quick and you can run past them before they react. Then you'll have more cover, and if you're lucky, the zombie with the RPG will do your work for you. If not, you should have just enough shot gun ammo to take them out with head shots. Collect as much gear as you can, and notice that you can carry a whole lot more that your limit. Once you're done with the zombies, you may be tempted to go hunting zombies for more loot. But there's plenty where we're going, so I recommend you head for the transition point.

    Agroprom

    If you used the southeast transition point, there are corpses on top of the hill with more loot in front of you. Walk between or around the two trees, up the slope and climb on top of the rocks to avoid the mutants. Also, avoid contact with Duty even though they show as neutral on your PDA. They turn on you without warning. There are some zombies to the west if you want more swag. Also, if you happen to gain stash coordinates, you can them for now and collecting them later in the game. Especially if the stash is near a Duty post.

    My strategy with collecting gear is to have something for every ammo type (except pistols, I only need one of those). If I get to a point where I have to decide between what to keep and what to drop, I always keep the most valued item. When I get to the Cordon I can trade these, get my gear fixed and upgraded. It's always good to have about six figures in your account. You'll have a chance to really build up your account once you get to the swamp. Head east to transport to the Garbage. Again, there's plenty of zombies to build up your inventory. At this point (without artifacts) you can carry 200 kilograms.

    From the plateau, head southeast to the Barracades by a train car. In the tower is a dead banit named Sheluden with the first PDA. Sheluden's PDA has some valuable information on flash drives and another PDA in the Underground, which we will get to later. Many of these flash drives tell about hidden transitions.

    Garbage

    Head for the transition point near the Receiving Outpost at the south end of the road. As you approach you'll hear a fire fight, which usually ends with an RPG blast. A squad of Russian Special Forces (RSF) is attacking some bandits. You can either way for the attackers to finish up and head toward the Gravel Pit, or try to take them out with an RPG as they leave. Either way, there's probably more loot than you can carry at this point.

    Be sure to save before you move on and check your PDA, because you never know who or what is going to be waiting for you when you spawn at the Far Outpost. I've been greeted by mutants and RSF. I was able to fight my way past the RSF, but the mutants forced me to go back through the Garbage, Dark Valley, and then to the Cordon. The object is to get to the Old Farmstead to trade off unwanted weapons and get some repairs, even some upgrades to the shot gun.

    Cordon North

    As you head south toward the Northern Farmstead in the Cordon there are mutants, a Controller, and two Burers on the right near the Bivouac in the Meadow and zombies to the left at the Camp in Spruce Forest. If you go left you can walk through the fence to get past the zombies. I haven't figured out how to kill the pseudogiants yet, so I try to avoid them. You may notice two transition points to the west of Hideout Under Rocks. These are described here.

    More corpses near Camp in spruce forest, but you'll have to deal with the zombies first. However, these are not armed and not very agressive. So, you may be able out maneuver them. Get to the Northern Farmstead so you can trade in all your goodies and get your gear repaired. You can take on optional missions, but there's no flash drives or PDAs in it for you. And unless you are really hard up for cash, these are a waste of ammo.

    Sheluden's PDA mentions a flash drive at the Quiet Hideout, which is east of the Old Farmstead. The corpse is guarded by some psuedodogs and at least one of them is a cloner. It's best to take them out from a distance before they start cloning. I should mention that clones can go through fences, too. The body of the RSF has a flamethrower and the Flash drive with data of hidden transition point on the Swamps.

    From the Quiet Hideout, go west to the Bonfire in Forest, but not to the transition point. There's a helicopter that likes to shoot rockets at you when you're in the Village Center. From the Bonfire in Forest, go up the hill to the railroad tracks and follow them west until you can get a good shot with an RPG. If you're luck enough to hit it in the tail section, it will go down with one shot. Otherwise, you're going to need a second hit. Once that's out of the way, follow the western edge of the map heading south towards the transition point in the southwest part of the map. As you pass the Bonfire Under Tree, be sure to stay on the east side of the fence. There's a hidden teleport up on the hill that we don't want to use just yet.

    Go straight to the transition point and do NOT stop at Sidorovich's. Go outside the fence when you get close to the tunnel so the military doesn't start shooting. Go into the tunnel and teleport to the Swamp.

    Swamp

    You spawn on the east side of the Southern Farmstead, which has a few Lone Wolfs you can either avoid, or take out. Then head for the Clear Sky camp. When you get south of the Fishing Village, walk through the fence and continue toward the camp. As you approach the gate, you'll be stopped by a rather length dialog, at which time you can choose how you want to play the rest of the game. My choice for this walkthrough is to join Clear Sky. Here are the objectives:

    • Find the Art-hunter and buy artifacts.
    • Find the way to Limansk.
    • Find all three Artifacts Of Death.
    • Collect 12 PDAs.

    I should also mention that I found playing the game with emissions on only slows the game down and offers no additional change your playing abilities, only to your patience. Also, Lebedev tells you about using Esc+F2, which brings up the Faction Commander control panel. Supposedly, it's a crucial part of the game. But I haven't figured out how it works to that end. Please feel free to enlighten me at kwag_myers@att.net.

    Note: There are two teleports in Clear Sky's camp. One is closed off and I haven't figured out how to get to it, and the other is in the tree next to the technician's hut, that takes you to random places, such as southwest of the Pump Station, or to the top of the Flea Market. To use it, go up the stairs of the hut, turn right and go up the ramp to the roof of the building east of the hut. Then, jump to the front part of the hut's roof and sprint jump at the tree.
    Note: I wasn't able to find a transition point in either the graves next to the old church or the graves just north of there. I looked at several locations for a small grave yard, but did not see one. However, the grave yard next to the church is the landing spot for the secret transition point behind the Duty base in the Agroprom. More information here.

    Don't forget to cash in on all those weapons you collected from the bandits and get your reward. Also, make sure your gear is in good shape. Novicov is the only technician that can install night vision on Clear Sky suits, so get it fitted before you leave the Swamp. Be sure to talk to the guide at the south end of the camp, Lotsman. He gives you some information on transition points (which I haven't quite figured out how to use yet). He also agrees to meet you in Limansk.

    Once you're ready to leave the camp, check your PDA for a red target west by northwest of the camp. This is Meatgrinder, and that's your next objective. After talking with him, head for the watchtower on the west edge of the map, where in the original Clear Sky, you were sent on your first mission. The guy in the tower tells you to run for the tower, then he climbs down and gets killed. Check the bodies because one of them has a really nice shot gun. You can hang around the Swamp, maybe take some jobs and build up your inventory of ammo and cash. You can by stashed from the trader and barkeep, but none of them have flash drives or PDAs.

    One thing I highly recommend is to head for the northwestern part of the Swamp which is marked Detector "Veles" on your PDA map. Remember that Veles behind the mattress in the original Clear Sky? Well, if not, there's a cave between the box cars and bridge, and behind the mattress in the corner is a Veles detector. See Hidden Veles Detector if you need a walkthrough. And be sure to check the cave for an artifact before you leave. You may pick up a Gravi for your belt. Also, you're probably going to encounter another helicopter flying over the northern part of the Swamp.

    I'm not sure about this next bit being necessary or optional, but it's fairly easy. Up in the northeast corner of the map, east of the Northern Farmstead, is a Person of Interest. This is Zarka, and she will ask you to escort her to her friends, which are northwest of the Encampment Under Embankment. Before you contact Zarka, you have to clear out the Northern Farmstead. When you do, be sure to watch for reenforcements coming in from the south west. If you look out the western facing window of the house you can see the bridge they use to get to the farm.

    Take Zarka to her friends (who may not actually be there, but it doesn't matter). Be sure to keep her on the minimap or she'll go back to her starting point. She stops by the pond just west of the Northern Farmstead to check for anomalies, then goes west until the fence turns north toward the train wreck. She passes the train wreck on the north side and meets up with her buddies just a little further west. After the dialog, they head to Agroprom, but don't bother trying to follow them.

    Be sure to collect as many artifacts as you can. Read the description of each one as some can be used as a secondary weapon. Put the artifact in place of a pistol or Bulldog 6 and hold the shoot key/button until you are ready to deploy. See Clear Sky Artifacts for a map of anomalies.

    When you're ready to move on, head for the transition point in the northeast corner of the map (where you met Zarka).

    Cordon South

    Hidden transition points: There is an unmarked transition point directly west of the Bondfire under tree, which is south of the Rookie camp, and takes you to the north edge of Dark Valley.

    You spawn in the Cordon just south of the Military Outpost. Walk on the east side of the fence until you get past the mine field. Go see Wolf in the Rookie village and take his job to escort a stalker named Anelia from the tunnel to the Dark Valley, which is marked Bonfire by Tunnel on you map. Anelia is trapped by three or four Bloodsuckers running around the camp. Then head for the Vehicle Station, where you have to take out the special forces there in order to complete the task. I've found it's easier at night because of their headlamps. On your way, make sure the Camp Near Van is clear of mutants. For some reason, Anelia holds up here if there's mutants present.

    My tactic for dealing with the bloodsuckers is to stand in the road just west of the rail fence. This seems to be their boundary for some reason an you can step forward a few steps to bait them to come at you. Remember to wait until they show themselves just before striking and take your shot at the head. It took a few tries, and a quick-save after each kill, but I was able to take them out one by one. Oh, and you have to give Anelia a gun before she will leave. Like Zarka, you must keep Anelia on your minimap as you escort her to the Rookie Camp, or you'll have to start the mission all over again.

    When you get back to the camp, Anelia tells you all about looking for her father and how her boyfriend came to the Zone to make some money for their wedding, but became a zombie instead. Let her tell her story because you get coordinates for a stash when she's done. In the stash is the Joker of Horror flash drive that tells you to look up Korsakov, who is in the Agroprom. But first, we need to collect some flash drives, PDAs, and artifacts, so back to the Garbage.

    Garbage 2

    In the Garbage, you get a message when you spawn about a toolbox stash (with a PDA inside), which is marked on your map (Forgotten PDA). Note that Bandits are now enemies. Head for the gas anomaly north of the Gravel Pit to pick up your two artifacts and then to the stash. From the stash, go east along the base of the trash heap and follow it around until you get to the New Encampment. Turn to the east and look for a dead tree with a large trunk. On the southeast side of the trunk is a corpse with the PDA from Garbage, which tells you of 12 PDAs you need to collect. There are more corpses north of this, if you want to loot them. Then head for the Flea Market.

    At the Flea Market, those bandits are waiting for you in the cellar again. You can shoot them through the door, and retrieve the Forgotten PDA. If the Flea Market is under attack from the north, it's a good idea to help them out because the attackers are packing some really nice hardware and you may be able to increase your arsenal, or at least pick up some ammo. There's also a couple bandits waiting in the cellar of the Flea Market to ambush you again. Approach carefully because they shoot through the door and it's best to come around from the southwest and stand at the end of the wall so you can lean to the right to take them out. In the cellar is the PDA of the Unknown.

    When you're finished collecting artifacts, head for the transition point east of the Flea Market to Dark Valley.

    Dark Valley

    Make your way northeast to Freedom's base if you need to get your equipment repaired. Starting from the northwest corner of the camp wall, go west to the fence and follow it north until, using your minimap, you are directly in line with the two corpses. Take out your detector and move in to grab the artifact, and loot the bodies if you want. Then, turn and go out the same way you came in. If you purchased any stashes from Ashot or the barkeeper and happened to get the Stalker's grave stash, there should be a nice little Goldfish artifact to increase your load capacity by 150 kg.

    Hidden transition point: North of the Stalker's grave stash that goes to the Bonfire under tree in the Cordon at first. However, at some point during the game it switches to the factory in Yantar.

    If you need any help with the stashes, see Clear Sky Stashes by Location - Dark Valley for walkthroughs.

    Make your way to the gas station and get the PDA from the corpse on top of the tank, which is (Uncle Artikular friend's PDA). This is where you went to get the flash drive off of Yar's friend in the original Clear Sky. If you need help getting the artifact from the top of the north tank, see the Note section for Gas Station. This PDA mentions a teleport in a tube at the Mercenary's base, which is marked on your map as the Factory Lobby. But if you try it too soon, the teleport is disabled. While you're still on top of the tank, take a look down the road south toward the bus. There may be a Burer in that area that is an easy shot from where you are.

    Next, head to the Encampment at Foothill, where you'll find the PDA los in Valley laying on a boulder behind the mattress.

    The first optional mission offered is to capture the Island southwest of the Factory Lobby. The only way I've found to deal with a Psuedogiant is with a Soul artifact that creates a Gravi anomaly. However, if one of the Freedom guys gets caught in the anomaly you create with the artifact, they turn on you and you can't complete the mission. So, I'm not sure if there's any value in the optional missions in Dark Valley as it may be a waste of time.

    There is a Controller in the Camp amidst some rocks. Standing on top of the hill north of the camp, he can be taken out with the Dragunov because you are out of his range (at this point in the game, I don't always have a .50 cal). Then continue collecting artifacts until you get to the south end of the map. There's another PDA in the Encampment on Farm, Lingov's corpse (in the original Clear Sky) in the southwestern corner of the map, which is now Commandant Glykin's PDA.

    The teleport behind the Factory lobby still doesn't work, so once you get all you can, head back to the Garbage and to the transition point north of the Northern Outpost. We need to find this Looter guy in the Mercenaries Bivouac.

    Army Warehouses

    Go north to a squad leader and ask for a job. He'll give you the Intelligent Service Commander's PDA task. Then check your map and find Looter and go talk to him in his...office. Looter tells you about hiring a former Clear Sky member who then shows on your map as an important person. You can buy some stashes from Looter, but none of them have any flash drives or PDAs.

    Before retrieving the PDA, there's a Controller northeast of the village that needs to be removed. I once encountered the Controller as I was walking straight from Looter to the PDA. He didn't see me and I was able to take him out before he did. I prefer to use the .50 cal on Controllers and Burers, but you can also use the Dragonov SVD, which takes a few shots, but it works. With the Controller down, switch to the shot gun and get up on the rocks on the west take out the mutants near the corpses. Try for the Chimeras first because they can jump and get you even on the tallest rock. I like to come at them from the west where there's a smaller rock I can jump up on and shoot between two larger ones. The Chimeras won't jump at you unless they can see you.

    With the mutants out of the way, grab the PDA and read it. You may even want to take a screen shot of it, because you have to return it in order to complete the mission and you can't take another job without completing this one. Before you leave the area, be sure to scan for artifacts. According to the PDA, one of the guys with Looter is a former Clear Sky member and has a PDA with valuable information on it. This is Butcher, whom Looter told you about, and is the blue icon on the east edge of the village on your map. Go talk with him, cap him, and take his PDA. Here's why:

    On one play through, I noticed his icon was gone and a corpse nearby. Sure enough, Butcher had bought it and he was carrying the O'consciousness Agent PDA. On another play through, I brought him an Artifact of Death, because he offered to tell me what to do with them, and he offered no help (maybe I need all three). Since the mission is to collect PDAs, I'm afraid Butcher has to take one for the team.

    The rest of the jobs here are optional. However, the second job just might result in a Barrett M82A1 .50 cal if you don't already have one, or you need some ammo. Or, you can just go get it without doing the mission. Again, be sure to scan the area to the north for artifacts after you aquire the item. There's a possible bug with the mission "Return the Jellyfish", where I didn't get credit for returning the item, but when I left the map, I got a message that the mission had failed.

    Looting corpses may get you the stash coordinates to the Cross near tree stash, which has some ammo for the Gravi gun. When you're ready to move on, head for the northwest transition point to the Red Forest. According to Looter, Luke's last known location was with Forester in the Red Forest. Before you jump, get either an RPG or .50 cal ready.

    Red Forest

    There may be a squad of zombies at the bend in the road up ahead who are about to attack Duty at the gate to the Limansk Reserve. If you have your RPG ready and get close enough, you can take out most of them with one shot. However, (depending on what level you're playing at) there's one Undefeatable Zombie that a .50 cal in the head before he goes down.

    Another possibility is you'll hear a swarm of mutant spiders. In this case, sprint to the shipping containers on the west side of Forester's building and jump on top. You can use your shot gun to clear them out. I've also had them come out right after taking out the last zombie. It's usually at this point that I turn my speakers off. Go in, talk to Forester and have him repair any equipment that needs it.

    Take caution when you leave the building as there may be any number of surprises waiting for you. So far, I've encountered a Burer in Forester's building, on the cat walk, and across the road (attacking the survivors of the zombie firefight). Once, he came into the room while I was talking to Forester and killed me before I knew it, then attacked the old man. I've seen it come down the ladder on the east side of the building a few times.     Jupiter Underground

    There's a group of zombies on the other side of the truck where you first spawn, plus a Pyrogeist roaming around. Follow the passage south carefully, using the vehicles as cover from the zombies along the way. When you come to where the passage branches to the right and dead-ends, another Pyrogeist starts at you, with a Psuedogiant right behind. There are two trucks back-to-back across the passage where you can jump up on top to deal with the Pyrogeist (I prefer the minigun, but a shot gun works, too). The Psuedogiant should be somewhere near the trucks so you can deploy another Gravi artifact to take it out. Just be sure you don't take yourself out, too.

    Stay on the left side walkway and jump up on the shipping container. There's a fence with three corpses and a few more further south. Only, they are really sleeping zombies that wake up as soon as you walk by them. Shooting them is a waste of ammo. Toss a grenade or two near the doorway on the left. This usually gets the invisible spiders going and you can use the shot gun on them. Also, by now you are probably in a Controller's Aura by now. When the way is clear, jump down the south side of the container, jump up on the next container and get your .50 cal ready for the Controller to come through the door. You may get a couple more spiders, too. Again, when the way is clear, jump down and sprint through the door and down two flights of stairs. There is usually one armed zombie along the way here, so be ready for it.

    At the bottom of the stairs, turn right and switch to the flamethrower as you sprint. Through the next door and turn right to follow the tracks on the right until the box car blocks the way, then move over to the left and continue sprinting. One option (if you're running low on flamethrower fuel) is to jump up on the locomotive and climb to the top where you can use a shot gun or minigun on the Psuedodogs, Snorks, and maybe even cap the Burer if it comes in from the next room. Whether you stop or not, follow the tracks into the next room until you pass two tanker cars on the right where there's a doorway just beyond. Once inside the doorway you are out of range for the Burer and Controller, but you probably have a few Psuedodogs on your trail. Cook them with the flamethrower and get ready for another group coming from the other way. Before you go through that door you should know that there's another Controller, probably at the top of the stairs. Duck inside the door and to the left to switch to the .50 cal. The Controller may even walk down the stairs, making it an easy head shot. This might be a good place to switch back to the flamethrower and Save.

    Up the stairs and into a flooded hallway with those giant rodents we first encountered in the Agroprom Underground. Stay in the open area at the top of the stairs and let the rodents come to you. At the end of the next hall go left and through the doorway straight ahead into a room with a crate containing Gravi Gun ammo and Save again. Out the door and around to the right are more spiders and two invisible bloodsuckers (one of them is carrying a Night Star artifact). The only way to see the bloodsuckers is to stand in water so you can see their footsteps. Northeast of the room you're in is a good spot where you can stand in front of the gate and throw a grenade to attract their attention. The only weapon that I've found to work on them is the shot gun. My strategy for the spiders is to lure them to the room with grenades where I cook them as they came through the door. You can also use a minigun (which tends to jam at this point) or RPG and keep retreating toward the large circular room until you get them all. At the end of the passage is a door on the left with an armed zombie waiting. If the zombie hasn't come out while you were dealing with the others, throw a grenade into the door to clear the way, then check to be sure you got him. He's only got a shot gun so he's not very dangerous.

    The way is mostly clear now and you can either go back to explore the area that you sprinted through or continue on and explore the room during your next visit. In the large circular room, there's a squad of Patrol Time who carry a semi-automatic Gauss and any mutants, Burer, Controller, etc. that you didn't deal with before. Also, there's an Exoskeleton in a crate that's located in a room south of the entrance to the overhead control room, which is on the west side of the circular room. This next part you may want to save for the next time through because the jump is a tricky one. Go to the platform with the generator on top (where you had to power up the generator to open the door in Call of Pripyat). Climb on top of the generator and either next to, or on top of the tank on the south end. Sprint-jump to the ledge west of the generator and go halfway up the ramp. Turn to the right and jump to the cat walk that leads to the Control Room. Inside the Control Room on the control panel is Zarka's PDA. When you're done exploring, return to the end of the passage and up the ladder to spawn back in Limansk.
